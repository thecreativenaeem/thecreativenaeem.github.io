What this helps with

Stop guessing who to message.

Most freelancers do not need 100 random DMs. They need a clear profile, a small list of right people, and messages that feel human.

  • Make your profile easy to understand before people reply.
  • Find people who may actually need your service.
  • Start conversations without begging or sounding spammy.
Before you start

Your profile is the page people check before they trust you.

Do this first. If your profile is unclear, even a good DM can get ignored.

1

Say who you help

Do not only write your job title. Tell people the type of client you help.

2

Say the result

Make the outcome simple. More calls, better content, better design, clearer offer, better profile.

3

Show proof

Add portfolio, examples, client work, useful posts, or anything that makes trust easier.

The roadmap

Follow this in order. Do not jump straight to pitching.

Step
1

Fix the first 5 seconds of your profile

A stranger should understand your work fast. Use this headline idea:

  • I help [type of client] get [clear result] with [your service].
  • Example: I help coaches get better LinkedIn leads with profile and content strategy.
  • Keep your banner simple: who you help, what result, and how to contact you.
Step
2

Pick one client type for now

Do not search for "clients". Search for one kind of person.

  • Coaches, consultants, agency owners, SaaS founders, ecommerce founders, local business owners, or creators.
  • Pick the group that is most likely to need your service.
  • You can change later. For now, focus makes the work easier.
Step
3

Search with words real people use

Go to LinkedIn search and test phrases like these:

  • "coach" "launching"
  • "consultant" "helping"
  • "agency owner" "looking for"
  • "founder" "building"
  • "new offer", "webinar", "hiring", "opening spots", "new website"
Step
4

Look for a real reason to message

Do not message only because they have a title. Find one useful signal first.

  • Their profile does not explain the offer clearly.
  • They post often but do not have a clear next step.
  • They are launching something new.
  • Their design, content, or profile does not match the level of service they sell.
Step
5

Warm up before you DM

Leave one useful comment before sending a message. Not "great post". Say something that adds to the topic.

  • Read their post properly.
  • Comment one useful point.
  • Write your observation in a sheet before you message them.
Step
6

Send a short message with one helpful idea

Start with what you noticed. Do not start with a big pitch.

  • Keep it short.
  • Make it specific to their profile, post, or page.
  • Ask if they want a quick example.
Step
7

Track replies and improve one thing

Do not judge everything from one message. Track your numbers weekly.

  • Profiles saved
  • Useful comments
  • Messages sent
  • Replies
  • Calls booked
Message scripts

Use these as a base. Make every message personal.

Copy the structure, not the exact words. The first line should come from something you actually noticed.

Profile clarity message

Hey [Name], I checked your profile after seeing your post about [topic]. Your content is clear, but your headline does not quickly say who you help and what result you create. Small suggestion: make the first line more outcome-based. Want me to send a quick example?

Design/profile message

Hey [Name], your offer looks strong. One thing I noticed: the banner and featured section do not feel as clear as the service you are selling. Small suggestion: make the banner explain the offer in one line. Want me to share a quick layout idea?

Content message

Hey [Name], I noticed your posts are getting engagement. Small thing: most posts end without a clear next step. A soft CTA could help interested people know what to do next. Want me to send 2 CTA examples?

Follow-up message

Hey [Name], quick example of what I meant: Instead of saying "I help businesses grow online", you could say: "I help [client type] get [specific result] with [service]." No pressure, just thought it may help.

Daily routine

Do this for 45 minutes a day.

This is enough to start. Keep it simple and repeat it.

15

Find people

  • Search one client type
  • Save 5 good profiles
  • Write one reason for each
15

Warm up

  • Read recent posts
  • Leave useful comments
  • Do not spam likes
15

Message and track

  • Send 3 to 5 DMs
  • Follow up with value
  • Update your sheet
