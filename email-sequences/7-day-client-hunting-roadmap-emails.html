Day 1: Make your profile easy to understand

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Before you search for clients, send DMs, or post anything, fix the thing people will check first: your LinkedIn profile.

If your profile is confusing, good prospects will not reply even if your message is good. They will click your name, look for 5 seconds, feel unsure, and leave.

Today we make your profile simple enough that a stranger can understand what you do without thinking hard.

Step 1: Use this headline formula

Your headline should not only say your job title. It should say who you help and what result you help them get.

Formula:

I help [type of client] get [clear result] with [your service]

Examples:

  • I help coaches get more booked calls with LinkedIn content and profile design
  • I help SaaS founders turn LinkedIn into a simple lead channel
  • I help ecommerce brands improve conversions with clean landing page design

If you are not sure yet, use a simple version for now. Clear beats clever.

Step 2: Fix your banner message

Your banner should answer one question: "Why should I care?"

Use this structure:

Main line: The result you help people get

Small line: Who it is for

Proof or CTA: DM me "audit" / Book a call / See featured work

Example:

LinkedIn profile + content system for freelancers Turn profile visitors into better client conversations DM me "audit" for a quick profile review

Step 3: Rewrite the first 3 lines of your About section

Most people write their About section like a resume. Don't do that.

The first 3 lines should speak to the client problem.

If your LinkedIn profile is getting views but not client conversations, the problem is usually not your skill. It is that your offer, profile, and content are not making the next step obvious. I help [client type] fix that with [your service/process].

Step 4: Add 3 useful things to Featured

Your Featured section should make trust easier.

Add any 3 of these:

  • A portfolio post
  • A case study
  • A free audit post
  • A client result
  • A service breakdown
  • A simple "how I can help" post

Your action for today

  1. Rewrite your headline using the formula.
  2. Update your banner message.
  3. Rewrite the first 3 lines of your About section.
  4. Add or improve 3 Featured items.

Do not overthink it. You can improve later. Today we just need your profile to stop confusing people.

Tomorrow we will choose who to hunt, because random searching creates random clients.

- Naeem

Day 2: Stop searching for "clients"
Preview text
Today we choose one client type so your search becomes simple.

Day 2: Pick one client type to hunt

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Today is where most freelancers make the first big mistake.

They search for "clients".

But "clients" is not a real search. It is too broad. You need a specific type of person or business that can buy your service.

If you target everyone, your profile becomes weak, your content becomes general, and your DMs sound random.

Step 1: Choose one client bucket

Pick one for the next 7 days. Not forever. Just for this training.

  • Coaches
  • Consultants
  • Agency owners
  • SaaS founders
  • Ecommerce founders
  • Local business owners
  • Course creators
  • Recruiters
  • Real estate professionals

Choose the one that has the best chance of needing what you sell.

Step 2: Write your simple hunting sentence

I am looking for [client type] who probably need help with [problem I solve].

Examples:

  • I am looking for coaches who need a clearer LinkedIn profile and better content.
  • I am looking for SaaS founders who are active on LinkedIn but their profile does not explain the product clearly.
  • I am looking for agency owners whose offer looks strong but their visuals look inconsistent.

Step 3: Search these exact phrases on LinkedIn

Go to LinkedIn search and try searches like these. Replace the words with your chosen client type.

"founder" "hiring" "coach" "launching" "consultant" "helping" "agency owner" "looking for" "SaaS founder" "building" "ecommerce founder" "scaling" "course creator" "webinar" "real estate" "agent"

Then click People. Open profiles that look active and relevant.

Step 4: Use LinkedIn filters

If you have filters available, use:

  • People
  • Location if your service depends on geography
  • 2nd connections if you want warmer people
  • Current company if you target a company type

Step 5: Save 20 profiles

Do not message yet. Today you only build the first list.

Make a simple sheet with these columns:

Name

Profile link

Client type

Why they might need help

Recent post/topic

Status

In the "why they might need help" column, write one real reason. Not a guess like "maybe client".

Example: "Banner does not say what they offer" or "Posting daily but no clear CTA" or "Launching a new offer".

Your action for today

  1. Pick one client type.
  2. Write your hunting sentence.
  3. Search 3-5 keyword phrases on LinkedIn.
  4. Save 20 profiles in a sheet.

Tomorrow we will separate normal prospects from people who may actually need help now.

That is where client hunting gets much easier.

- Naeem

Day 3: Find people who may need help now
Preview text
Not every profile is worth messaging. Today we look for buying signals.

Day 3: Look for buying signals

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Yesterday you made a list of possible prospects.

Today we make that list better.

Most freelancers message people only because they match a title. That is weak.

A better way is to look for signals. A signal is a clue that someone may need your help soon.

Signal 1: They are launching something

Search their recent posts for words like:

launching new offer new website new brand new service opening spots waitlist webinar masterclass new product

If you sell design, content, LinkedIn help, websites, funnels, ads, or strategy, launches matter. Launches create urgency.

Signal 2: They are posting but the message is unclear

Check their last 5 posts.

Ask yourself:

  • Can I understand what they sell in 10 seconds?
  • Do their posts connect to one clear offer?
  • Is there a clear next step?
  • Are people commenting but not asking about the service?

If they are active but unclear, that is a strong signal.

Signal 3: Their profile and offer do not match

This is common.

Maybe their posts talk about a premium service, but their banner looks basic. Maybe their headline says one thing, but their About section says another.

Write the mismatch in your sheet.

Example: "Posts about helping founders, but headline only says content strategist."

Signal 4: They recently changed direction

Look for posts where they say:

I am now helping... I changed my offer... I started working with... I am focusing on... I am building... I am taking on...

People changing direction often need help with positioning, content, visuals, profile, website, or outreach.

Signal 5: Their audience is engaging

If someone gets comments, they already have attention. Your job is to check if that attention is turning into business.

If the comments are mostly "great post" and not real buying conversations, there may be a positioning problem.

Score your list

Add a new column in your sheet called Signal Score.

Give each person a score:

  • 1 = weak fit
  • 2 = possible fit
  • 3 = strong fit

Only message 2s and 3s later. Do not waste time on 1s.

Your action for today

  1. Open the 20 profiles from yesterday.
  2. Check profile, banner, headline, About section, and last 5 posts.
  3. Write one real signal for each good prospect.
  4. Score each person 1, 2, or 3.
  5. Keep your best 10.

Tomorrow we will warm them up before sending a message.

This small step makes your DM feel less random.

- Naeem

Day 4: Warm them up before the DM
Preview text
Do this for 10 minutes before messaging. It makes you look human.

Day 4: Warm up your prospects

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Today is simple, but most people skip it.

Before you DM someone, let them see your name in a normal way.

Not by spamming likes. Not by writing "great post". Real engagement.

Step 1: Pick your best 10 prospects

Use yesterday's list. Choose the people with the best signal score.

Open each profile and check if they posted in the last 30 days. If they are not active at all, keep them for later. Active people are easier to start with.

Step 2: Leave one useful comment

Find a recent post where you can say something real.

Use this comment formula:

Point they made + your useful addition + simple human ending

Examples:

This is a strong point, especially the part about making the offer easy to understand. A lot of people try to fix content before fixing the profile, but the profile is what people check after the post.
I like this. One thing I would add is that the CTA should match the buyer stage. If the person is still problem-aware, "book a call" can feel too early. A softer CTA can work better.
This is useful. The biggest issue I see with freelancers is they know their skill, but they do not explain the business outcome clearly enough. That small shift changes the whole profile.

Step 3: React to their story or post only if it makes sense

Do not force it. One useful comment is enough.

Your goal is not to become their fan. Your goal is to enter their world like a normal person.

Step 4: Write your observation before the DM

For each prospect, write one sentence in your sheet:

I noticed [specific thing] and I think [simple improvement/opportunity].

Examples:

  • I noticed your posts are strong, but your headline does not say who you help.
  • I noticed your banner promotes the podcast, but your offer is about coaching.
  • I noticed your content gets engagement, but there is no clear next step for interested people.

What not to do

  • Do not like 15 posts at once.
  • Do not comment "great value".
  • Do not pitch in the comments.
  • Do not pretend you know them.

Your action for today

  1. Pick your best 10 prospects.
  2. Leave one useful comment on each active prospect.
  3. Write one clear observation for each person.
  4. Do not DM yet unless the conversation naturally starts.

Tomorrow we send the first message.

And because you did the homework, your message will not sound like a copy-paste pitch.

- Naeem

Day 5: Send this kind of DM
Preview text
No pitch slap. Start with a real observation and make it easy to reply.

Day 5: Send a value-first message

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Today you send the first DM.

The goal is not to close a client in one message.

The goal is to start a real conversation with someone who has a real reason to care.

The rule

Do not start with your service.

Start with what you noticed.

People ignore generic pitches, but they pay attention when you show you actually looked.

DM formula

Hey [Name], I noticed [specific thing].

Small suggestion: [useful idea].

Want me to send a quick example?

This works because it is short, specific, and easy to reply to.

Use these scripts

For LinkedIn/profile help:

Hey [Name], I checked your profile after seeing your post about [topic]. Your content is clear, but your headline does not quickly say who you help and what result you create. Small suggestion: make the first line more outcome-based instead of role-based. Want me to send a quick example?

For content strategy:

Hey [Name], I noticed your posts are getting decent engagement, but most of them end without a clear next step. Small suggestion: add a soft CTA that matches the post, not just "DM me". Want me to send 2 CTA examples for your style of content?

For graphic design:

Hey [Name], I looked at your profile and your offer looks solid. One thing I noticed: the banner and featured section do not feel as premium as the service you are selling. Small suggestion: make the banner explain the offer in one line and use the featured section as proof. Want me to share a quick layout idea?

For website/landing page:

Hey [Name], I checked your page quickly. The offer is interesting, but the first section could explain the result faster. Small suggestion: lead with the pain/result before explaining the features. Want me to send a simple hero section structure?

How many to send

Send 5 to 10 messages today. Not 100.

If you send too many too fast, quality drops and you start sounding robotic.

Update your sheet

Add these statuses:

  • Commented
  • DM sent
  • Replied
  • Follow-up needed
  • Call booked
  • Not a fit

Your action for today

  1. Choose 5 to 10 warmed-up prospects.
  2. Use one script, but customize the first line.
  3. Ask permission before giving a bigger suggestion.
  4. Track every message in your sheet.

Tomorrow I will show you how to follow up without sounding desperate.

That part matters because many good replies happen after the first message.

- Naeem

Day 6: Follow up like a normal person

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

If someone does not reply, it does not always mean no.

Maybe they were busy. Maybe they saw it and forgot. Maybe your timing was off.

But your follow-up should not sound like pressure.

Wait time

Wait 2 to 3 days after the first DM before following up.

Do not send "??" or "just following up" with no value.

Follow-up 1: Add the example

If your first DM asked permission to send an example, your follow-up can include a small example.

Hey [Name], quick example of what I meant: Instead of: "I help businesses grow online" You could try: "I help [client type] get [specific result] with [service]" It makes the profile easier to understand in the first few seconds.

Follow-up 2: Make it easy to say yes or no

No pressure by the way. I just noticed it and thought it might be useful. If improving this is not a focus right now, all good.

This removes pressure. People trust you more when you are not chasing.

If they reply "sure"

Send the useful idea. Keep it short.

Nice. I would start with the headline. Right now it says: [their current headline] I would test something closer to: [your improved version] Reason: it says who you help + the outcome faster. That makes it easier for the right person to understand why they should keep reading.

If they reply "thanks"

Do not pitch immediately. Ask a simple question.

Glad it helped. Are you currently trying to get more client conversations from LinkedIn, or is LinkedIn more of a visibility channel for you right now?

If they say they are working on it

Now you can move toward a call.

Makes sense. If you want, I can take a quick look and point out the 2-3 things I would fix first. If it looks useful after that, we can talk about whether it makes sense to work together.

Your action for today

  1. Check who did not reply from Day 5.
  2. Send follow-up 1 only to the best-fit people.
  3. If someone replies, do not rush into a pitch.
  4. Ask one question to understand their situation.

Tomorrow is the final lesson.

We will turn replies into calls and build the simple daily routine you can keep using.

- Naeem

Day 7: Turn the system into calls

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Today we connect everything.

Client hunting is not one magic message. It is a small daily system:

  1. Clear profile
  2. Specific client type
  3. Buying signals
  4. Useful engagement
  5. Value-first DM
  6. Simple follow-up
  7. Clear next step

When to suggest a call

Do not suggest a call just because they replied.

Suggest it when one of these happens:

  • They ask how you would fix it.
  • They say they are working on that problem.
  • They ask what you do.
  • They share more details about their situation.
  • They say your suggestion makes sense.

Call invite script

That makes sense. From what you shared, I think the first things to look at are: 1. [specific issue] 2. [specific issue] 3. [specific issue] If you want, we can do a short call and I can show you what I would fix first. If it feels useful, we can talk about next steps.

If they ask for price too early

It depends on what needs to be fixed. I do not want to throw a random price without understanding the situation. If you share what you are trying to improve, I can tell you what level of help makes sense.

If they are not ready

All good. I would still fix [one useful thing] first. That alone should make your profile/content clearer. If you work on it later and want a second pair of eyes, just message me.

This keeps the door open without chasing.

Your daily 45-minute routine

Use this after the training ends:

10 minutes: Search for new prospects

10 minutes: Check signals and save the best ones

10 minutes: Leave useful comments

10 minutes: Send 3-5 personalized DMs

5 minutes: Update your sheet and follow up

Your tracking numbers

Do not judge the system by one message. Track weekly numbers.

  • Profiles added
  • Useful comments left
  • DMs sent
  • Replies
  • Calls booked
  • Clients closed

After 2 weeks, improve the weakest part.

If nobody replies, improve your targeting or first message.

If people reply but calls do not happen, improve your questions and call invite.

If calls happen but clients do not close, improve your offer and sales conversation.

Your action today

  1. Send call invites to warm replies.
  2. Build your 45-minute daily routine.
  3. Track your numbers for the next 14 days.
  4. Keep improving one part at a time.

You now have the full roadmap.

Simple work. Done daily. With real attention to the person in front of you.

That is how client hunting starts working.
